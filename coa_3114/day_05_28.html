May 28: Paestum

Paestum, or Poseidonia, is the city of Poseidon in southern Italy. Like Taras, Paestum was a colonial Greek city in the Magna Grecia. A significant amount of the city remains preserved. Over time, this colony changed from being a Greek colony to a Latin colony, due to the separation from mainland Greece, and the colonists independance from the rest of Greece and other colonies. Unlike other colonies, Paestum would adopt the culture of the locals into their culture, thus the language and lifestyle of the colonists here became polluted in the eyes of the Greeks on the mainland.

There are several remarkable temples here. Hera I is unique for being a 9 x 18 Nonastyle temple. This odd number of columns most likely has its origins in archaic temples. Next to Hera I, Hera II, which is most likely a temple to Apollo, contains two tiered columns on the inside and three different sizes of columns through the whole temple. The exterior columns have 24 flutes, the interior columns on the lower lever have 20 flutes, and the interior columns on the upper level have 16 flutes. There is also a temple to Athena here, which had 6 layers of space from direct sunlight to the inside of the temple cella where there was almost no light.
